# Brand Positioning Decision Log

**Date**: 2026-03-15
**Participants**: Product Owner (Artem), Brand Designer (Alfredo, agent #15)

---

## Brand Personality Statement

> Grida is the sharp, confident tool that brings geometric precision to a chaotic physical
> world. It doesn't shout — it commands attention through craft. Every square corner, every
> bold line, every emerald accent says the same thing: *someone cared enough to make this
> beautiful, even here.*

**Status**: Approved

---

## Brand Attributes

| Attribute | What it means | What it does NOT mean |
|-----------|--------------|----------------------|
| **Bold** | Confident, decisive, unapologetic visual presence | Not aggressive, loud, or overwhelming |
| **Precise** | Every pixel intentional, structured, grid-aligned | Not rigid, cold, or clinical |
| **Modern** | Tech-forward, current, YC-era aesthetics | Not trendy for trend's sake |
| **Crafted** | Attention to detail, designed with care | Not over-decorated, not ornamental |
| **Clear** | Instantly readable, zero ambiguity, clean hierarchy | Not minimal to the point of emptiness |

**Status**: Approved

---

## Brand Archetype

**Primary**: The Creator — obsessed with making something beautiful and functional where
nobody expected it. Proves operational software can be a design object.

**Secondary**: The Magician — transforms chaos (messy warehouse loading) into order (clean
digital grid).

**Status**: Approved

## Brand Voice

| Dimension | Grida's voice | Notes |
|-----------|--------------|-------|
| Formal ↔ Casual | **Casual-professional** | Smart colleague, not corporate memo |
| Technical ↔ Plain | **Plain first** | "15 orders loaded" not "15 items ingested" |
| Warm ↔ Neutral | **Warm** | Friendly but not chatty. No exclamation avalanche |
| Confident ↔ Humble | **Confident** | "Your shipment is ready" — self-assured, knows what it's doing |

**Status**: Approved

## Tagline

### Candidates Considered

1. "Order in every load." — Clean, double meaning. Rejected: too subtle.
2. "Load with precision." — Action-oriented. Rejected: generic, any logistics tool could say this.
3. **"The grid sees everything."** — Bold, mysterious, memorable. Selected.
4. "Craft meets cargo." — Brand story in 3 words. Rejected: too abstract.

### Decision: "The grid sees everything."

**Rationale**: It has personality. Slightly mysterious, slightly bold. Positions Grida as an
all-seeing control center — one calm, precise screen that sees the whole chaotic loading
process. The kind of tagline that gets screenshotted and shared on Product Hunt.

**Status**: Approved
