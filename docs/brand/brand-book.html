Brand Guidelines — Version 1.0 — March 2026

01 — Brand Story

An island of craft in chaos

Truck loading is loud, physical, messy. The warehouse floor doesn't care about design. But Grida does. Grida is the calm, precise, beautifully crafted control center in the middle of all that chaos.

Someone cared enough to make this beautiful, even here.

What Grida is

A logistics management platform that brings geometric precision to truck loading. It tracks orders, maps trailer walls, and gives operators total visibility into every shipment. Every pixel is intentional.

Brand Personality

Bold

Confident, decisive, unapologetic presence

Precise

Every pixel intentional, grid-aligned

Modern

Tech-forward, YC-era aesthetics

Crafted

Attention to detail, designed with care

Clear

Instantly readable, zero ambiguity

02 — Logo

Logo System

The Grida logo consists of two assets: the Scoped G mark (primary) and the Grid G (favicon). The horizontal lockup pairs the mark with the full wordmark.

Primary Mark — Scoped G

Corner brackets framing a geometric G. All bars at equal 6px weight. Used at sizes 32px and above.

Favicon — Grid G

Standalone G without brackets. Used for browser tabs, app icons, and sizes under 32px.

Horizontal Lockup

Scoped G mark + full "Grida" wordmark. The wordmark is self-sufficient. Zalando Sans Expanded, weight 500, baseline-aligned to the inner G.

Grida
Grida

Clear Space

Minimum clear space around the logo equals the height of the G mark within the icon. No text, graphics, or borders within this zone.

Logo Usage Rules

Do

Use the provided SVG files without modification

Place on solid white or dark green-black backgrounds

Use favicon variant for sizes under 32px

Don't

Add rounded corners, shadows, or glow effects

Rotate, stretch, or skew the logo

Change the bar proportions or bracket angles

Use the Scoped G at sizes under 32px

04 — Typography

Type System

Two fonts from the same family, different widths. Headings feel expansive and bold. Body feels clean and compact. Zero visual friction between them.

Zalando Sans Expanded
Headings, buttons, badges, tabs · Weights 500, 600, 700 · Page titles, card titles, buttons, badges, nav tabs
Zalando Sans
Body · Weights 400, 500 · Body text, labels, inputs, table cells, descriptions

Type Scale

Page Title — 48px / 700
Section Title — 32px / 600
Card Title — 20px / 600
Button — 14px / 500 / Expanded
Body — 15px / 400
Label — 13px / 500 / Regular
Overline — 11px / 600 / Expanded / uppercase

Usage Rules

Do

Use Expanded for headings, buttons, badges, tabs, brand name

Use weight 500 (Medium) for bold body text

Self-host via @fontsource (GDPR, performance)

Don't

Use Expanded for body paragraphs or input values

Use weight 700 for body (too heavy)

Load from Google Fonts CDN in production

05 — Shape

Shape & Structure

Everything is square. Grids are squares. Grida is squares. Bold borders define structure. Shadows are replaced by borders.

Border Radius

0px
All components
8px
Never
Full
Never

Border Weight

TierWidthElements
Tier 12pxCards, buttons, inputs, dialogs, badges, sheets
Tier 21pxTable rows, wall cells, separators, dropdown items
Tier 3 (Accent)4pxActive tab indicators, progress bars, emphasis lines

Shadows

No box-shadows on cards. Flat + bold border. Shadow implies softness. Grida is sharp. Only exception: focus ring for accessibility.

Alignment

Left-align by default. Text, headings, form labels, logos, and content blocks sit on the same left edge. Center alignment is the exception, not the rule.

Do

Left-align logo, subtitle, and form fields to the same edge

Left-align page titles, card titles, body text

Anchor content to a consistent left margin

Don't

Center-align form headers while left-aligning inputs

Mix alignment directions within the same content block

Center body text or labels

06 — Voice

Voice & Tone

Grida speaks like a smart colleague — confident, warm, plain. Never like a corporate memo. Never like it's unsure of itself.

Voice Dimensions

Formality
Casual-professional. "Your shipment is ready."
Not corporate. Not "Dear User, we wish to inform you..."
Technical
Plain first. "15 orders loaded."
Not jargon. Not "15 items ingested into the pipeline."
Warmth
Friendly but not chatty. One exclamation mark max.
Not cold. Not "Error 403." Not "Yay!!! Amazing!!!"
Confidence
Self-assured. "Shipment complete."
Not hedging. Not "We think your shipment might be ready."

Copy Examples

Grida says

"Shipment created."

"All orders loaded."

"Save failed. Try again."

"Welcome."

Grida doesn't say

"Shipment was successfully created!!!"

"Dear User, loading has been completed."

"Error: SAVE_FAILED_ERR_500"

"Hey friend! So glad to see you! 🎉"
