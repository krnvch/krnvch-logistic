Concept 01
The Window

8 squares in a 3×3 grid with an empty center. The center is the eye of the grid — the viewpoint from which "the grid sees everything." The filled cells frame the central opening, like a window into order. Immediately reads as "grid." Works at any size.

Concept 02
Grid G

The letter G constructed from bold perpendicular bars — like architecture, not typography. Five rectangles: top bar, left bar, bottom bar, right bar (bottom half), and the crossbar shelf. In the horizontal lockup, the G-mark replaces the first letter: [G]rida. No curves. Pure grid construction.

Concept 03
The Signal

Two corner brackets — top-left and bottom-right — framing empty space with geometric precision. Like alignment marks in photography or registration marks in print. Says "focus," "precision," "the grid is watching." The diagonal tension between the two L-shapes creates movement within structure.
